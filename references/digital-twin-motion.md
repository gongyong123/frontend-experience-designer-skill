# Digital Twin Motion

Use stronger motion only where it helps spatial understanding, operational awareness, or incident response.

## Equipment Selection

When selecting equipment:

- Ease camera toward the target.
- Add a persistent outline or glow.
- Reveal the data card after the target is visually identified.
- Keep the selected equipment visible and unobscured.

Good sequence:

1. Resolve target equipment.
2. Fly camera to a stable inspectable angle.
3. Apply highlight.
4. Open related metrics or card.

## Alarms

Use alarm motion to attract attention without creating noise.

- Warning: amber pulse.
- Critical: red blink or stronger glow.
- Acknowledged: reduce to steady outline.
- Cleared: fade highlight out.

Avoid fast flashing. Keep blink frequency readable and safe.

## Data Flow

Represent flow direction and status clearly.

- Power: directional line pulse or glow along cable/busbar.
- Cooling/airflow: translucent particles or animated streamlines.
- Water: blue directional flow along pipe.
- Network: small packets or traveling dots.

## Simulation

For simulation states:

- Use color fields, particles, or overlays to show the result.
- Keep legends visible.
- Animate changes between scenarios.
- Do not obscure equipment identity while showing simulation data.

## 3D Cards

For spatial data cards:

- Anchor the card to the selected object or projected screen position.
- Fade/scale in; do not pop abruptly.
- Reposition smoothly as camera moves.
- Hide or collapse cards when too many overlap.
